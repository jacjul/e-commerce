# Key Financials

## Market Cap
- calculates the value of Stocks with the number of stocks

## Enterprise Value 
- calculates with the $EV = Market cap + Debt - Cash - Cash Equivalents $
- this is the amount it would need to buy the company

## Revenue 
- is the total generated money through sales, interest and investments

## EBITDA (Expenses before Intest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization)

- calculates operating profitability by excluding financing(interest), taxes, non-cash (depreciation amortization)
$$ EBIDTA = NetIncome +Interest+Taxes+Depreciation +Amortization$$
- Ignores Financing, CapEx
- EBITDA margin = $ \frac{EBIDTA}{TotalRevenue}x100$
- often between 10-20% considered decent above 20% good

- Rule of 40%: EBIDTA-margin + Revenue Growth > 40%

## Free Cashflow

- is the remaining cash after operational expenses are covered and capital expenditures(investments in assets) 
- represents money to pay debt, dividends or reinvest

$$ Free CashFlow = Operating CashFlow-CAPEX $$
or $ EBIDTA - Taxes- Change in Capital-CAPEX$

- good is considered FCF Margin with 15-20 % 
- $$ \frac{FCF}{Revenue} x100

- or FCF Yield with the Market Price per Share
- $$ \frac{\text{FCF per Share}}{\text{MarketPriceperShare}} or \frac{\text{FCF}}{\text{Market Capitalization}}
# Valuation and Quality 

## Price-Earnings-Ratio (P/E-Ratio)
- Shows what an Investor has to pay for 1 $ of Earnings
- calculates the Marketvalue per share to the Earnings per share
- it exists the P/E trailing and P/E forward -> trailing uses the Earnings of the last 12 months while forward gives a prognose of the upcomming 12 months
- so a diclining P/E from trailing to forward actually means that the costs per earnings are shrinking or that the earnings are growing
- does not concern the debt levels or growth rates directly
 $$P/E = Marketvalue per Share/Earning per Share$$
 
 - should be compared to others in the same industry field
 - low P/E can mean its undervalued or that it has low growth perspective
 - between 10/20 is considered good, to low eg. 5 can be actually be dangerous

 ## Price-to-Book (P/B-Ratio)

 - Calculates the Marketvalue per share to the Bookprice per share
 - Bookvalue contains the net assets(shareholder equity) hence $TotalAssets-TotalLiabilities$
 - a low P/B can mean undervaluation, a high P/B high growth expectations or overvaluation
 - best for capital-intensive-industries (Banks,Manuafacturing)
 - less usable for Brands(name) or technology
 - should be under 2.0 -1.25  but only can be used to compare companies of one industrie

## Revenue Growth 

- shows the growth of revenue hence sells in total over a specific period
- measured quaterly or annualy 
- can show volume increase or price increase or expand to new markets
$$ (CurrentRevenue- PastRevenue)/PastRevenue  $$

- above 10% considered healthy; 15-25% seen as excellent
- start-ups often need way higher growth rates while mature businesses can live with 5%- 9%
- should outperform market growth rate

## Profit margin

- Calculates the Net Income to Revenue Ratio 
$$ \frac {\text{Net Income}}{Revenue} x 100 $$
- high margin good efficency 

## ROE 

- Return of Equity (ROE) compares the Net Income with the Shareholder Equity(Total Assets-Total Liabilities)
- it shows how good Equity is used to generate Income
$$ ROE = \frac{NetIncome}{ShareholderEquity} x100$$
- High ROE >15% suggests efficient management
- Low ROE > 10%
- a way to lower ROE is by stock buybacks (eg. APPLE with 150%)
- Comparisson has to be made with other Competitors and with past years

## Debt to Equity

- It shows how much a company depends on Liabilites/Debt in comaparison to equity/ own funds
- higher value equals higher risk
$$ DtE = \frac{TotalLiabilities}{Total ShareholdersEquity}$$
- Ratio of 1.5 means for every 1$ of equity 1.5 $ of Debt
- Good is 1- 1.5

## Beta 

- Compares volatility of the Stock to the rest of the market eg. S&P500
- Value above 1.0 means stock is more volatile then market
- stock with high betas are often preferred in bull markets while low betas are preferred in bear markets
